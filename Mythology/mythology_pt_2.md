
# Mythology Bootcamp

## Greek Mythology

### Olympian Gods and Their Symbols

- **Zeus** - thunderbolt, aegis, eagle; affairs: Leda (Helen, Castor, Pollux), Alcmene (Hercules), Europa, Io, Ganymede; dethroned Kronos
- **Hera** - peacock, pomegranate; queen of gods, goddess of marriage; constantly persecutes Zeus's illegitimate children
- **Poseidon** - trident, horses, earthquakes; created first horse; contest with Athena for Athens; Medusa connection (raped her in Athena's temple)
- **Demeter** - wheat sheaf, torch; Persephone's mother; caused winter when Persephone abducted
- **Athena** - owl, olive tree, aegis, spear; born fully armored from Zeus's head; patron of Athens; helped Perseus, Odysseus
- **Apollo** - lyre, laurel wreath, sun chariot, silver bow; god of music/prophecy/sun; Oracle at Delphi; Daphne, Cassandra stories
- **Artemis** - bow and arrow, moon, deer; twin of Apollo; goddess of the hunt; Actaeon story
- **Ares** - spear, vulture, dog; god of war; affair with Aphrodite; generally disliked by other gods
- **Aphrodite** - dove, rose, myrtle, scallop shell; born from sea foam; married Hephaestus, affair with Ares; Paris and the golden apple
- **Hephaestus** - hammer, anvil, fire; god of forge; threw off Olympus (two versions); made Achilles' armor, Hermes' winged sandals
- **Hermes** - caduceus (winged staff with snakes), winged sandals, winged helmet; messenger god; psychopomp (guides souls to underworld); caduceus is NOT the medicine symbol (that is Rod of Asclepius)
- **Dionysus** - grapevine, thyrsus, leopard; god of wine and madness; Maenads, Orpheus death connection
- **Hades** - helmet of darkness (invisibility), bident, Cerberus; not technically Olympian (rules underworld); Persephone abduction

### Creation Myth and Titanomachy

- **Chaos** came first, primordial void
- From Chaos: Gaia (earth), Tartarus (abyss), Eros (love), Erebus (darkness), Nyx (night)
- Gaia + Uranus (sky) produced Titans, Cyclopes, Hecatoncheires
- Uranus feared his children and stuffed them back into Gaia; Gaia gave Kronos a sickle to castrate Uranus; Aphrodite born from sea foam
- **Kronos** swallowed his children (Hestia, Demeter, Hera, Hades, Poseidon) fearing prophecy of being overthrown
- **Rhea** hid baby Zeus in Crete, gave Kronos a stone wrapped in cloth
- Zeus grew up, returned, forced Kronos to regurgitate siblings
- **Titanomachy**: 10-year war, Zeus's side vs Titans; Zeus freed Cyclopes (who gave him thunderbolt) and Hecatoncheires; Titans imprisoned in Tartarus
- Atlas punished separately, forced to hold up the sky

### Prometheus, Fire and Pandora

- Prometheus created mankind from clay
- Stole fire from Olympus and gave it to humans
- Punishment: chained to rock, eagle ate his liver daily (regenerated each night); eventually freed by Hercules
- Zeus's revenge: **Pandora**, first human woman, given a jar (not box, mistranslation) containing all evils; opened it releasing suffering; only Hope remained inside

### Important Stories

- **Persephone and Hades**: abducted while picking flowers; ate pomegranate seeds (6 seeds = 6 months underground); explains seasons; Eleusinian Mysteries were secret religious rites based on this myth
- **The Three Fates (Moirai)**: Clotho (spins thread of life), Lachesis (measures it), Atropos (cuts it); even gods could not override them
- **Icarus**: Daedalus built wings of feathers and wax; flew too close to sun despite warnings; also know Daedalus built the Labyrinth for Minos
- **Eros and Psyche**: Psyche so beautiful Aphrodite grew jealous; sent Eros to make her fall for a monster; Eros fell for her himself; series of impossible tasks set by Aphrodite; eventually Psyche becomes immortal
- **Orpheus and Eurydice**: greatest musician; Eurydice died of snakebite; Orpheus descended to underworld, charmed Hades with music; given Eurydice on condition he not look back; looked back; lost her forever
- **Oedipus**: prophecy he would kill father and marry mother; abandoned as infant, raised by Corinthian king; unknowingly killed father Laius; solved Sphinx's riddle; married mother Jocasta; blinded himself on discovering the truth; Sophocles wrote this
- **Pan and Syrinx**: Pan chased nymph Syrinx; she turned into hollow reeds; Pan made the pan flute from them
- **Arachne**: mortal weaver who challenged Athena; wove tapestry mocking the gods; Athena destroyed it; Arachne hanged herself; Athena transformed her into a spider; origin of "arachnid"
- **Bellerophon and Pegasus**: tamed Pegasus with golden bridle from Athena; killed Chimera; tried to fly to Olympus; Zeus sent gadfly to sting Pegasus; Bellerophon fell back to earth
- **Leda and Zeus**: Zeus appeared as swan; Leda laid eggs; children: Helen of Troy, Clytemnestra, Castor and Pollux (Dioscuri)
- **Narcissus**: so beautiful he fell in love with his own reflection; Echo (cursed to only repeat others' words) loved him hopelessly; he wasted away staring at himself

### Tragic Love Stories

- **Pygmalion**: sculptor fell in love with his own ivory statue; Aphrodite brought her to life (Galatea)
- **Narcissus and Echo**: see above
- **Eros and Psyche**: see above
- **Daphne and Apollo**: Apollo mocked Eros; Eros shot Apollo with gold arrow (love) and Daphne with lead arrow (repulsion); Apollo pursued Daphne; she begged her father river god Peneus to save her; transformed into laurel tree; Apollo adopted laurel as his symbol
- **Orpheus and Eurydice**: see above
- **Hero and Leander**: Leander swam the Hellespont nightly to visit Hero; drowned in storm; Hero threw herself into the sea

### The Trojan War

**Causes**
- Eris (discord) threw golden apple "for the fairest" at a wedding
- Hera, Athena, Aphrodite each claimed it
- Paris of Troy chosen as judge (Judgment of Paris)
- Each goddess bribed him: Hera offered power, Athena offered wisdom, Aphrodite offered the most beautiful woman; Paris chose Aphrodite
- Helen (wife of Menelaus, King of Sparta) taken to Troy by Paris

**Key figures**
- **Agamemnon**: commander of Greek forces, brother of Menelaus
- **Achilles**: greatest Greek warrior; vulnerable only at heel; argued with Agamemnon over Briseis; withdrew from battle; returned after Patroclus killed by Hector; killed Hector; killed by Paris (arrow to heel, guided by Apollo)
- **Hector**: greatest Trojan warrior; killed Patroclus; killed by Achilles; body dragged around Troy's walls
- **Odysseus**: cleverest Greek; devised Trojan Horse
- **Patroclus**: Achilles' companion; wore Achilles' armor; killed by Hector
- **Cassandra**: Trojan princess; Apollo gave her prophecy then cursed her to never be believed; predicted Troy's fall; ignored
- **Ajax**: second greatest Greek warrior after Achilles
- **Aeneas**: Trojan hero; escaped Troy; founded Rome (Virgil's Aeneid)

**Key events**
- War lasted 10 years
- Achilles dipped in Styx by his mother; invulnerable except where she held him (heel)
- Trojan Horse: giant wooden horse, Greeks hidden inside; Trojans brought it in despite Cassandra's warnings and Laocoon's warnings (he and his sons killed by sea serpents)
- Fall of Troy: Greeks emerged at night, opened gates, sacked city



## Indian Instruments

**Stringed (Plucked)**
- Sitar - Ravi Shankar, Nikhil Banerjee
- Sarod - Amjad Ali Khan, Ali Akbar Khan, Allauddin Khan
- Veena - Rudra Veena (Hindustani), Saraswati Veena (Carnatic)
- Tanpura - drone instrument, four strings
- Santoor - hammered dulcimer, Shivkumar Sharma (adapted for classical)

**Stringed (Bowed)**
- Sarangi - "hundred colors", Ram Narayan
- Violin - adapted into Carnatic, MS Gopalakrishnan

**Wind**
- Bansuri - bamboo flute, Krishna association, Hariprasad Chaurasia
- Shehnai - Bismillah Khan

**Percussion**
- Tabla - Zakir Hussain, Allah Rakha; dayan + bayan
- Mridangam - Carnatic equivalent of tabla
- Pakhawaj - dhrupad style

**Framework**
- Natya Shastra categorization - Tantu/Ghana/Sushir/Avanaddha Vadya
- Hindustani vs Carnatic split

## Regional Music

**Classical**
- Hindustani (North) - raga, tala, gharana system, improvisation-heavy; forms: Dhrupad, Khayal, Thumri, Tappa
- Carnatic (South) - composition-based, devotional; Trinity: Tyagaraja, Muthuswami Dikshitar, Syama Sastri
- Key difference: Hindustani absorbed Persian/Arabic influence, Carnatic didn't

**North**
- Bhangra/Giddha - Punjab, harvest festival
- Ragini - Haryana folk songs
- Kajri, Chaiti - UP, seasonal songs
- Maand - Rajasthan, royal court music
- Baul - Bengal mystic minstrels (UNESCO intangible heritage)

**South**
- Carnatic vocal - Kriti form, Bharatanatyam connection
- Villupattu - Tamil Nadu, bow-song storytelling
- Oppana - Kerala Muslim wedding music
- Yakshagana - Karnataka, musical theatre tradition

**West**
- Lavani - Maharashtra, often erotic/satirical
- Garba/Dandiya - Gujarat, Navratri
- Powada - Maharashtra, ballad tradition

**East**
- Chhau - folk music-dance of Jharkhand/Bengal/Odisha
- Odissi music - tied to Jagannath temple tradition

**Northeast**
- Bihu - Assam, three types (Rongali, Kongali, Bhogali)
- Sattriya - Assam, monastery tradition (UNESCO)
- Zeliang - Nagaland tribal music

**Sufi/Devotional**
- Qawwali - Sufi, Nusrat Fateh Ali Khan connection
- Kabir tradition - Nirguni bhajans, pan-North India
- Bhakti movement - Mirabai, Tukaram, Kabir

## Temple Run

**Char Dham**
- Badrinath (Uttarakhand), Dwarka (Gujarat), Puri (Odisha), Rameshwaram (Tamil Nadu)
- Chota Char Dham - Kedarnath, Badrinath, Gangotri, Yamunotri (all Uttarakhand)

**12 Jyotirlingas**
- High yield: Somnath (Gujarat), Kashi Vishwanath (Varanasi), Mahakaleshwar (Ujjain), Kedarnath, Rameshwaram, Trimbakeshwar (Nashik), Omkareshwar (MP)

**South - Dravidian architecture**
- Brihadeeswarar - Thanjavur, Raja Raja Chola, UNESCO
- Meenakshi Amman - Madurai, Pandya dynasty
- Ranganathaswamy - Srirangam, largest temple complex in India
- Nataraja - Chidambaram, Chola capital
- Tirumala Venkateswara - Tirupati, richest temple in world by donations
- Gangaikonda Cholapuram - Rajendra Chola
- Shore Temple - Mahabalipuram, UNESCO, Pallava dynasty

**North - Nagara architecture**
- Kashi Vishwanath - Varanasi, Jyotirlinga, Gyanvapi controversy
- Kedarnath - Uttarakhand, 2013 floods
- Khajuraho - MP, Chandela dynasty, erotic sculptures, UNESCO
- Vaishno Devi - J&K, cave temple, Trikuta hills
- Amarnath - J&K, ice Shivalinga, forms and melts naturally

**West**
- Somnath - Gujarat, destroyed and rebuilt repeatedly
- Dwarkadhish - Gujarat, Chalukya architecture
- Siddhivinayak - Mumbai, Ganesha
- Dilwara - Mount Abu, Jain, marble architecture

**East**
- Jagannath Puri - Odisha, Rath Yatra, Char Dham
- Konark Sun Temple - Odisha, "Black Pagoda", UNESCO, chariot shape
- Kamakhya - Guwahati, Shakti Peetha, tantric tradition
- Dakshineswar - Kolkata, Hooghly river, Ramakrishna Paramahamsa
- Lingaraj - Bhubaneswar, oldest temple in city

**clever title**
- 51 Shakti Peethas - where Sati's body parts fell
- Ashtavinayak - 8 Ganesha temples, all Maharashtra
- Pancha Bhuta Stalas - 5 Shiva temples representing 5 elements, all Tamil Nadu
- Cave temples - Ajanta, Ellora (Kailasa temple, largest rock-cut), Elephanta

**Architecture dynasties**
- Chola - Brihadeeswarar, Nataraja, Gangaikonda
- Pallava - Shore Temple, Mahabalipuram
- Chandela - Khajuraho
- Rashtrakuta - Kailasa at Ellora
- Hoysala - Belur, Halebidu (Karnataka)
- Vijayanagara - Virupaksha (Hampi), UNESCO
